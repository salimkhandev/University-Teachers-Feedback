ABSTRACT

The University Teachers Feedback System is a comprehensive, AI-powered web application designed to streamline the collection, analysis, and visualization of student feedback regarding university faculty members. Traditional feedback mechanisms in educational institutions often suffer from inefficiencies such as paper-based processes, lack of anonymity, delayed results, absence of actionable insights, and minimal engagement between teachers and the feedback they receive. This system addresses these challenges by providing a digital platform where students can submit feedback on their teachers through an intuitive user interface, administrators can monitor the entire ecosystem, and teachers can receive AI-generated summaries, interactive rating visualizations, and chatbot coaching based on student comments. The system employs a modern technology stack comprising React with Vite for the frontend, Node.js with Express for the backend, MongoDB with Mongoose for data persistence, and Google Gemini artificial intelligence for natural language processing tasks. Role-based access control ensures that students, teachers, and administrators each have appropriate views and permissions. The platform incorporates intelligent caching strategies to optimize AI API usage, CSV bulk import capabilities for rapid data population, and comprehensive analytics dashboard with statistical charts. This thesis presents the complete system architecture, database schema design, implementation decisions, feature specifications, testing strategies, and deployment considerations of this production-ready educational technology solution.

CHAPTER 1: INTRODUCTION

1.1 Overview

In the contemporary landscape of higher education, the quality of teaching directly influences student learning outcomes, institutional reputation, and overall academic excellence. Universities worldwide have recognized the critical importance of collecting and analyzing student feedback as a mechanism for continuous improvement in teaching quality. However, the process of gathering, processing, and deriving actionable insights from student feedback has traditionally been fraught with challenges that span administrative, pedagogical, and technological domains. The University Teachers Feedback System is an AI-powered web-based solution designed to automate and enrich this process. Utilizing modern web development frameworks combined with state-of-the-art Large Language Models (LLMs), the system provides a digital platform where students can anonymously and securely evaluate their assigned teachers. Simultaneously, it empowers faculty members with detailed analytics and AI-generated text summaries of student qualitative comments, closing the feedback loop effectively within educational institutions.

1.2 Motivation

Historically, many universities have relied on paper-based feedback forms distributed at the end of each academic term. These paper-based systems present numerous inefficiencies: they are resource-intensive to print and distribute, require manual data entry for analysis, are susceptible to data loss, and provide limited anonymity which discourages honest student responses. The logistical burden of managing thousands of paper forms across multiple departments is substantial, requiring significant staff time. Furthermore, the time lag between feedback collection and result dissemination often renders the insights less actionable, as the academic term may have already concluded by the time teachers receive their evaluations. Even when institutions have transitioned to basic digital solutions, many systems offer little more than simple form submission and quantitative rating aggregation. These systems fail to extract meaningful insights from the qualitative comments that constitute the richest source of feedback data. The inability to automatically analyze text-based feedback represents a significant missed opportunity, as student comments contain nuanced observations about teaching methodology, communication effectiveness, and classroom management that cannot be captured through numerical ratings alone. The motivation behind this project is to build an intelligent, secure, and end-to-end automated platform that leverages Large Language Models—specifically Google's Gemini model—to solve both quantitative and qualitative feedback bottlenecks. This system transforms raw student comments into coherent, unbiased, and actionable development goals for faculty, while providing admins with high-level dashboards to monitor institutional performance.

1.3 Problem Statement and Description

Educational institutions face several critical challenges in implementing effective teacher feedback systems: First, there is a total lack of integrated platforms. Institutions often use disjointed tools: students submit on one form, admins aggregate results in Excel, and teachers receive static PDF reports weeks later. This fragmentation leads to data inconsistencies and delays. Second, feedback analysis is overwhelmingly manual. Processing hundreds or thousands of student comments to extract meaningful patterns, identify recurring complaints, and summarize strengths/weaknesses requires prohibitive human effort. Consequently, qualitative text data is often left unanalyzed. Third, the feedback loop remains broken. Students do not see any visible impact of their feedback, and teachers receive raw feedback dumps without structured, constructive guidance, leading to defensive reactions rather than pedagogical improvement. Fourth, system configuration and bulk population are highly complex. Managing university organizational structures—comprising departments, semesters, sections, subjects, and assignments—manually is prone to error, yet modern evaluation tools often lack seamless import capabilities. The University Teachers Feedback System addresses these problems by providing an integrated, role-based platform that automates administrative setup via CSV imports, aggregates ratings instantly into dashboards, and applies AI models to summarize written comments and act as a conversational teaching coach.

1.4 Proposed Solution

The proposed solution is a modular, responsive Single Page Application (SPA) integrated with a secure RESTful API and a Generative AI backend. The solution is tailored for three distinct user roles: Students: Access a lightweight portal displaying their specific teachers and subjects based on section/semester. They can easily submit ratings (1-10) and written comments, and edit their submissions (with version tracking) as long as the evaluation window remains open. Teachers: Access a comprehensive dashboard featuring Recharts-based visualizations of their rating distributions and subject averages. They receive an AI-generated, synthesized summary of student comments, and can interact with a "Chat Coach"—an AI chatbot primed with their actual feedback data that offers personalized, constructive teaching guidance. Administrators: Oversee the platform using overview stats, automated ranking charts, student tracking lists, and a multi-step System Setup Wizard. Admins can bulk-import student records via CSV with automated conflict resolution and configure AI API settings, including key rotation and model selection.

1.5 Goal and Objectives

The primary goal of this project is to design, implement, and deploy an AI-enhanced student feedback platform that bridges the gap between evaluation, analysis, and faculty coaching. To achieve this, the following objectives have been defined: 1. To implement role-based access control (RBAC) ensuring secure, distinct interfaces and permissions for students, teachers, and administrators. 2. To develop an intuitive feedback submission engine that allows students to rate and comment on teachers, with full update capability and auditing. 3. To integrate Google Gemini AI models for automatic generation of teacher performance summaries from raw student comments. 4. To design an intelligent chatbot coaching interface that allows teachers to converse with their feedback data safely. 5. To provide administrators with granular tracking of student feedback progress, flagging pending submissions across sections and departments. 6. To create a multi-step Setup Wizard including bulk import of student and teacher rosters from CSV files with automatic conflict resolution. 7. To implement intelligent caching for AI-generated summaries to minimize API latency and transaction costs. 8. To deploy the system using a high-availability, modern architecture on cloud platforms (Vercel and MongoDB Atlas).

1.6 Scope of Project

The scope of the project encompasses the development and integration of the following modules: Domain Configuration: Modeling university departments, semesters, sections, subjects, and teacher-subject-section-semester assignments. Authentication Module: JWT-based secure session management featuring access/refresh token rotation, bcrypt password hashing, and login interfaces. Student Portal: Progress tracking, teacher card grids, and inline rating sliders with comment validation. Teacher Portal: Subject-wise analytics, interactive rating distribution charts, AI summary panel, and the conversational AI Coach. Admin Portal: Overview metrics, system setup wizard (Department, Semester, Section, Subject, Teacher, Assignment, Student, and CSV Bulk Import), user accounts CRUD, AI settings key management (rotation, masking, deactivation), and admin-teacher chat report features. The scope is limited to the web application domain (responsive for mobile and desktop). While student identities are tracked internally to prevent duplicate submissions, student names and roll numbers are completely stripped from feedback records before they are displayed to teachers, maintaining anonymity.

1.7 Limitations of Project

The project is subject to the following technical and operational limitations: External API Dependency: The AI-powered summary and chat services rely on the availability of Google's Gemini API. Network latency or upstream service interruptions can affect performance. API Quota Limits: Generation of summaries and chat responses is bound by Gemini rate limits (requests per minute). While mitigated by multi-key rotation and caching, extreme usage may result in temporary throttling. Internet Connectivity: As a cloud-hosted platform utilizing MongoDB Atlas and external AI services, the system requires active internet connectivity. Natural Language Limits: The AI engine parses qualitative data in English. Non-English comments are aggregated but may not be summarized with the same granularity.

CHAPTER 3: REQUIREMENT ENGINEERING

3.1 Business Requirements

The University Teachers Feedback System must address the core business needs of educational institutions for collecting, analyzing, and acting upon student feedback. The system must provide a digital platform that replaces paper-based feedback processes, enables automated analysis of qualitative comments through AI, and delivers actionable insights to faculty members while maintaining student anonymity. The system must support role-based access for students, teachers, and administrators, and provide comprehensive dashboards for monitoring institutional teaching performance.

3.2 User Requirements

Students require an intuitive interface to view their assigned teachers, submit feedback with ratings and comments, track their completion progress, and edit submissions within the evaluation window. Teachers require detailed analytics showing rating distributions, subject-wise performance metrics, AI-generated summaries of student comments, and an interactive chat interface for personalized coaching. Administrators require system-wide dashboards for monitoring teacher rankings, tracking student feedback completion, managing user accounts, configuring system settings through a setup wizard, and managing AI API keys and model selection.

3.3 System Requirements

The system must be a web-based application accessible through modern browsers, supporting both desktop and mobile devices. The system must use a secure authentication mechanism with JWT tokens and role-based access control. The backend must provide RESTful APIs for all operations, integrate with Google Gemini AI for natural language processing, and use MongoDB for data persistence. The system must support CSV bulk import for student data, implement caching for AI-generated content, and provide real-time updates for feedback submission status.

3.4 Functional Requirements

Authentication and Authorization: FR1: The system shall support login using username and password credentials. FR2: The system shall issue JWT access tokens and refresh tokens for session management. FR3: The system shall implement role-based access control distinguishing students, teachers, and administrators. FR4: The system shall automatically refresh expired access tokens using valid refresh tokens. FR5: The system shall protect all API endpoints based on user roles.

3.5 Non-Functional Requirements

Performance: NFR1: API response times should be under 200ms for non-AI endpoints. NFR2: AI summary generation should be cached to reduce latency to under 50ms for cached responses. NFR3: The system should handle concurrent users without significant degradation. NFR4: Database queries should be optimized with appropriate indexes.

3.6 Domain Requirements

The system must model the educational domain including departments, semesters, sections, subjects, teacher assignments, and student enrollments. The system must enforce business rules such as one teacher per subject per section, unique student roll numbers per department, and prevention of duplicate feedback submissions. The system must support hierarchical data relationships where departments contain semesters, semesters contain sections, sections contain subjects, and subjects are assigned to teachers. The system must maintain academic year information for roll number generation and support semester-based data partitioning.

3.7 Use-case Descriptions

Use Case 1: Submit Feedback

Actor: Student Description: A student submits feedback for an assigned teacher including a rating (1-10) and a mandatory comment. Preconditions: Student is logged in, teacher assignment exists, feedback window is open Main Flow: Student views their assigned teachers on the dashboard, selects a teacher to provide feedback, adjusts rating slider to desired value (1-10), enters comment in the text area, clicks submit button, system validates rating and comment are present, system saves feedback to database, system updates progress indicator, system displays success message. Postconditions: Feedback is saved, progress is updated, student cannot submit again for same assignment

Use Case 2: View AI Summary

Actor: Teacher Description: A teacher views an AI-generated summary of their student feedback. Preconditions: Teacher is logged in, feedback exists for the teacher Main Flow: Teacher navigates to AI Summary tab, system checks if cached summary exists, if cached and current feedback count matches, display cached summary, if new feedback exists, show "stale" indicator, teacher clicks "Generate Summary" if stale, system collects all feedback data, system sends data to Gemini AI with summary prompt, system receives and displays generated summary, system caches summary with current feedback count. Postconditions: Summary is displayed and cached for future use

CHAPTER 7: CONCLUSION AND FUTURE RECOMMENDATION

7.1 Conclusion

The University Teachers Feedback System successfully addresses the critical challenges faced by educational institutions in collecting, analyzing, and acting upon student feedback. By leveraging modern web technologies and artificial intelligence, the system provides a comprehensive solution that transforms the traditional feedback process into an automated, insightful, and actionable experience. The system achieves all stated objectives including role-based access control, automated AI-powered analysis of qualitative comments, interactive coaching through chat interfaces, comprehensive admin dashboards, and efficient bulk data management. The implementation demonstrates robust software engineering practices with clear separation of concerns, modular architecture, and comprehensive testing. Key achievements of the project include: - Successful integration of Google Gemini AI for natural language processing - Implementation of intelligent caching strategies to optimize API usage and costs - Development of a multi-key rotation system for high availability - Creation of an intuitive, responsive user interface supporting dark/light themes - Comprehensive role-based security with JWT authentication - Efficient CSV bulk import with automatic conflict resolution - Real-time progress tracking for feedback completion

7.2 Future Recommendation

While the current system provides a robust foundation for teacher feedback management, several enhancements could be considered for future iterations: 1. Multi-language Support: Extend the AI integration to support feedback analysis in multiple languages, making the system accessible to institutions with diverse linguistic populations. 2. Mobile Application: Develop native mobile applications for iOS and Android to provide students with convenient on-the-go feedback submission capabilities and push notifications for deadline reminders. 3. Advanced Analytics: Implement predictive analytics to identify teaching trends, correlate feedback with student performance metrics, and provide early warning systems for teaching quality issues. 4. Integration with Learning Management Systems: Develop APIs to integrate with popular LMS platforms like Canvas, Blackboard, and Moodle for seamless data synchronization and automated student enrollment. 5. Sentiment Analysis Enhancement: Implement fine-grained sentiment analysis to detect specific teaching aspects such as communication skills, subject knowledge, classroom management, and accessibility. 6. Peer Review System: Add functionality for teachers to request peer observations and feedback, creating a comprehensive 360-degree evaluation system. 7. Historical Trend Analysis: Implement longitudinal tracking of teacher performance over multiple semesters to identify improvement patterns and professional development needs. 8. Real-time Feedback: Enable live feedback collection during lectures through mobile devices, providing immediate insights for teaching adjustment. 9. Export and Reporting: Add comprehensive reporting capabilities with PDF export, customizable dashboards, and scheduled report generation for institutional review. 10. Notification System: Implement email and SMS notifications for feedback deadlines, summary availability, and important system updates.

These enhancements would further strengthen the system's value proposition and expand its applicability across diverse educational contexts.
